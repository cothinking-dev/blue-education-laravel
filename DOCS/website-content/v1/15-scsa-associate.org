** What Is SCSA?
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Two-column — text left, logo/badge right
:LAYOUT: 60/40 split. Right: SCSA logo and WA Dept of Education badge
:END:

The School Curriculum and Standards Authority (SCSA) is the governing body responsible for Kindergarten to Year 12 curriculum, assessment, standards, and reporting in Western Australia.

SCSA oversees the Western Australian Certificate of Education (WACE) — the qualification that Year 12 students complete before entering university.

Blue Education has been officially recognised by SCSA, under the WA Department of Education, to promote this curriculum internationally.

** The Vision Behind It
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Two-column — text left, image right
:LAYOUT: 55/45 split
:IMAGE: Diverse classroom of students engaged in learning
:END:

You run a school. You want to give your students every possible advantage — a globally recognised qualification, a pathway to world-class universities, an education that prepares them for an international future.

But sending students abroad is expensive, complicated, and not possible for every family.

What if you could bring the education to them instead?

The School Curriculum and Standards Authority (SCSA) governs the K-12 curriculum in Western Australia — including the Western Australian Certificate of Education (WACE), the qualification Year 12 students complete before entering university. It's rigorous, internationally recognised, and accepted for direct entry into Australian universities.

As an official SCSA Associate, Blue Education helps schools like yours understand, apply for, and deliver this curriculum — bringing Australian education standards into your classrooms, wherever in the world they are.
