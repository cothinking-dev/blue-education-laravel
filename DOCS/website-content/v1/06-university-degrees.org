** Why an Australian Degree?
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Alternating two-column — 3 rows
:LAYOUT: Text/image alternating sides
:END:

*** Global recognition
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: World map with highlighted connections
:END:

Australian qualifications are regulated by the Tertiary Education Quality and Standards Agency (TEQSA). This government-backed quality assurance means your degree is respected by employers and institutions in every major economy. Programs in regulated professions — such as teaching, nursing, and engineering — are aligned with Australian professional registration requirements.

*** Industry connections
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: Students in industry placement / internship setting
:END:

Australian universities maintain strong ties with industry. Many programs include practical components, industry placements, and projects with real organisations — giving you experience alongside your qualification.

*** A career launchpad
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: Young professional in Australian city
:END:

Your degree doesn't just end at graduation. The Post-Study Work Visa gives you 2–4 years to gain Australian work experience, build your professional network, and position yourself for permanent residence.

** Why an Australian Degree?
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Alternating two-column — 3 rows
:LAYOUT: Text/image alternating sides
:END:

*** Your degree travels with you
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: World map with highlighted connections
:END:

Every Australian university is regulated by the Tertiary Education Quality and Standards Agency (TEQSA) — a government body that exists solely to ensure quality. That's why Australian qualifications are recognised and respected in every major economy. Your degree works wherever you go. And if you're pursuing a regulated profession — teaching, nursing, engineering — your program is aligned with Australian professional registration requirements.

*** You don't just learn — you work
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: Students in industry placement / internship setting
:END:

Australian universities don't teach in a vacuum. Many programs include industry placements, real-world projects, and professional mentoring. By the time you graduate, you've already built connections and experience that matter to employers.

*** Graduation isn't the end — it's the beginning
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: Young professional in Australian city
:END:

The Post-Study Work Visa gives you 2–4 years of open work rights in Australia after graduation. That's time to gain local experience, build a professional network, and position yourself for permanent residence. For many of our clients, this is where the real journey begins.
