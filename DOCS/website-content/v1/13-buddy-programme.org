** What Is the Buddy Programme?
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Two-column — text left, image right
:LAYOUT: 55/45 split
:IMAGE: Group of students on field trip, laughing
:END:

The Buddy Programme is a 14-day educational and cultural immersion designed for international students who want to experience Australia before committing to long-term study.

You don't just visit Australia. You live it. Mornings are spent in structured English lessons. Afternoons are spent in real Australian classrooms, sitting alongside local high school students, studying the same subjects, making real connections.

You stay with a carefully selected homestay family who provides meals, support, and a genuine window into Australian life.

It's not a tour. It's a trial run for your future.

** A Letter to Parents
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Two-column — text left, image right
:LAYOUT: 55/45 split
:IMAGE: Parent watching child walk into school building, seen from behind

We know what it takes to let them go.

Your child is about to board a plane to a country they've only seen in pictures. They'll be nervous. You'll be more nervous. You'll wonder if they'll be safe, if they'll eat properly, if they'll be okay without you.

Here's what we've learned after 25+ years of running this programme: they'll surprise you. In 14 days, they'll navigate a new school. They'll sit in classrooms where everything is in English. They'll help in the community. They'll come home with stories, friendships, and a confidence you haven't seen before.

The Buddy Programme isn't a holiday. It's the moment your child discovers what they're capable of.

** What Their Days Look Like
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Two-column split — Morning / Afternoon
:LAYOUT: Side-by-side cards, icon-topped
:END:

*** Mornings: Finding Their Voice
:PROPERTIES:
:ICON: sun
:END:

Structured English lessons, but nothing like a textbook class. They learn about Australian history, geography, and wildlife through conversation and interaction. They build vocabulary, practise grammar, and gain the confidence to express themselves in English — not perfectly, but bravely.

*** Afternoons: Becoming Part of the School
:PROPERTIES:
:ICON: users
:END:

This is where the magic happens. Your child joins Australian high school students in their regular classes — science, mathematics, all of it. They sit at the same desks. They work on the same problems. They eat lunch together. By day three, they have a buddy. By day ten, they have a friend.
