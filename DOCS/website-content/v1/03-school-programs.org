** The Australian School System
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Two-column — infographic left, text right
:LAYOUT: 45/55 split. Left: visual diagram of 13-year school system
:END:

Australia's school system spans 13 years:

- *Preparatory year* — Foundation/Kindergarten
- *Primary* — Years 1–6 (or 1–7 in some states)
- *Secondary* — Years 7–12 (or 8–12 in some states)
- *Compulsory* until age 16 in most states and territories

Both public and private options are available, with consistently high standards regulated by state and federal authorities. As an SCSA Associate, Blue Education holds official recognition from the WA Department of Education, giving us unique insight into the Western Australian curriculum (WACE).

** What We Handle
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Feature list — icon + heading + description
:LAYOUT: 2-column grid, 6 items
:END:

*** School Selection & Placement

Every child is different. We take the time to understand your child's strengths, interests, and needs — then match them with a school where they'll feel at home.

*** Student Visa (Subclass 500)

Visa paperwork for minors can be especially stressful. We manage the entire Subclass 500 application — documentation, health checks, character requirements — so nothing falls through the cracks.

*** Accommodation

Your child will live with a vetted homestay family through the Australian Homestay Network (AHN). Meals included. These are families who genuinely care about their students' wellbeing.

*** Guardianship

Australian law requires legal guardianship for unaccompanied students under 18. We arrange this through Professional Student Care Australia or International Student Alliance — trusted providers we've worked with for years.

*** Welfare Monitoring

We don't just place your child and walk away. We conduct ongoing welfare checks, maintain regular contact with the school, and keep you informed every step of the way — all in compliance with Department of Home Affairs requirements.

*** Cultural Adaptation

Moving to a new country at a young age takes courage. We help your child adjust to Australian school life, social norms, and daily routines — so the transition feels supported, not overwhelming.
