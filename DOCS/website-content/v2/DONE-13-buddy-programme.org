*** Headline

Two weeks. Real school. Real families. Real Western Australia.

*** Subheadline

High school students join Australian classrooms, live with vetted homestay families, and explore Western Australia through structured field trips — all within a supervised 14-day programme run in partnership with Anglican Schools.

-----

** What Is the Buddy Programme?
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Two-column — text left, summary table right
:LAYOUT: 55/45 split
:IMAGE: Group of students on field trip, laughing
:END:

*** Body

Two weeks inside Australian school life. Morning sessions focus on English and Australian culture. Afternoons are spent in regular high school classes, alongside local students. Homestay accommodation — with meals provided and homestay families approved through police background checks — ensures the experience is safe, supported, and authentic.

*** Programme Summary

| Detail         | Info                          |
|----------------+-------------------------------|
| Duration       | 14 days                       |
| Age group      | High school students          |
| Accommodation  | Homestay (meals included)     |
| School partner | Anglican Schools              |
| Certificate    | Certificate of completion     |
| Voluntary work | Yes, through local councils   |

** Your 14 Days
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Two-column split — Morning / Afternoon
:LAYOUT: Side-by-side cards, icon-topped
:END:

*** Mornings: English Language & Culture
:PROPERTIES:
:ICON: sun
:END:

Structured lessons built around Australian context — history, culture, geography, and wildlife. Grammar, vocabulary, and sentence structure taught practically, not in isolation.
- Australian history, geography, and wildlife as lesson themes
- Vocabulary, grammar, and sentence structure
- Communication skills developed through interactive class discussion

*** Afternoons: High School Integration
:PROPERTIES:
:ICON: users
:END:

Students join regular Australian high school classes — Science, Maths, and other subjects, all in English. The point is participation, not observation: same lessons, same teachers, same expectations as local students.
- Standard academic classes alongside Australian peers
- All subjects taught in English (including Science, Maths)
- Social and cross-cultural skills developed in a real school environment

** Beyond the Classroom
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: BentoBox — 3 cards with images
:LAYOUT: 3-column grid, image-topped cards
:END:

*** Homestay Experience
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: Family dinner table, warm lighting
:END:

Police-cleared homestay families provide meals, emotional support, and a genuine share of Australian family life — including weekend activities. Students are looked after, not just housed.

*** Local Field Trips
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: Students at outdoor landmark, coastal WA
:END:

The classroom comes to life outside it. Programme destinations include Caversham Wildlife Park, Rottnest Island, The Pinnacles, King's Park, the Fremantle Market, and a Penguin and Sea Lion Cruise — each chosen to connect to the cultural and environmental themes taught in class.

*** Voluntary Work
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: Students helping at community event
:END:

Students who want to go further can arrange voluntary work with local councils. The voluntary work certificate has practical value — it strengthens CV and scholarship applications for students intending to continue their studies.

-----

** Why It Matters
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Feature list — icon + heading + one-liner
:LAYOUT: Vertical list, alternating background rows
:END:

- *Test before you commit* — experience Australian education without a multi-year enrolment
- *Build real English skills* — practical, immersive language development in 14 days of genuine classroom use
- *Strengthen applications* — voluntary work certificate and programme certificate support future university and scholarship submissions
- *Adapt, not just observe* — participating as a student, not a visitor, builds the kind of independence that carries forward
- *Make connections that last* — with Australian classmates and homestay families in a supervised environment
- *Anglican Schools partnership* — established pastoral care, vetted homestay families, and a programme that has run with the same school network since its founding
