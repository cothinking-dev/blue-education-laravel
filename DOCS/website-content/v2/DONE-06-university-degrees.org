** Our Partner Universities
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: University grid — WA institutions highlighted, with note on broader partners
:LAYOUT: Desktop: logo + name + descriptor grid. WA universities featured prominently. Mobile: 2-column wrap. Each card links to the relevant university profile on the Our Partners page.

Blue Education works with all five main Western Australian universities — and a wider network of institutions across Australia. Your advisor matches you to the right one based on your goals, background, and intended field.

*** The University of Western Australia (UWA)

One of Australia's leading research universities. Strong in medicine, science, law, and engineering. Consistently ranked in the global top 100.

*** Curtin University

Perth's largest university, with particular strength in engineering, technology, business, and health sciences. Strong industry connections and work-integrated learning across most programmes.

*** Murdoch University

Known for health sciences, law, veterinary science, and environmental studies. Smaller cohorts and a strong focus on student support.

*** Edith Cowan University (ECU)

Recognised for creative arts, education, nursing, and security studies. A practical, career-focused institution with strong ties to Western Australian industry.

*** The University of Notre Dame Australia

A smaller, values-based university with strengths in health, law, philosophy, and education. Located in Fremantle.

*** Global recognition
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: World map with highlighted connections
:END:

Western Australia's universities are regulated by TEQSA, ensuring consistent academic standards across all programmes. Qualifications are recognised across major economies — including in regulated professions such as teaching, nursing, and engineering.

*** Industry connections
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: Students in industry placement / internship setting
:END:

Many programmes include industry placements, research projects, and professional mentoring. Graduates leave with demonstrated experience in their field — not just a transcript.

*** Post-study work rights
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: Young professional in Perth city
:END:

The Post-Study Work Visa (Subclass 485) gives graduates 2 to 4 years of open work rights in Australia. That's time to build experience in your field, establish professional connections in Perth, and consider your options for permanent residence.

** Fees & Costs
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Text block — muted background
:LAYOUT: Full-width, centred text, no more than 3 short paragraphs.
:END:

Tuition fees vary by institution, field of study, and qualification level. Most universities require fees to be paid per semester or trimester before each study period begins.

As an international student, your fees are separate from your living costs. Perth is widely regarded as one of Australia's most liveable and cost-effective cities for students — your advisor can provide a detailed cost breakdown for your specific situation.

** How to Apply
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Numbered steps — horizontal or vertical
:LAYOUT: Desktop: 5 horizontal steps connected by arrows. Mobile: vertical stacked list. Each step has a number, short heading, and one sentence.
:END:

1. *Assess your eligibility* — Your advisor reviews your academic background, English level, and career goals to confirm which programmes and institutions suit your situation.
2. *Choose your institution and programme* — Blue Education identifies the right match from our partner institutions and presents your options.
3. *Submit your application* — We manage the application process and liaise with the institution on your behalf.
4. *Receive your offer and apply for your student visa* — Once you have a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE), your visa application can proceed. [[/services/migration/student-visas][Learn about student visas →]]
5. *Arrive, enrol, and begin* — Your advisor remains available throughout your studies and into your post-graduation planning. [[/services/career][Explore career services →]]
