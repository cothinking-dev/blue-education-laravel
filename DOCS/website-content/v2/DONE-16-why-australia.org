** Five Reasons
:PROPERTIES:
:SECTION: Alternating two-column — numbered, text/image alternating sides
:LAYOUT: 5 rows, each with large number, heading, body text, and image
:END:

*** 01. Globally recognised qualifications
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: University lecture hall, diverse students
:END:

Nine [[/services/education/degrees][Australian universities]] rank in the QS World University Rankings top 100 — six of them in the top 50. Every institution is regulated by TEQSA, Australia's national quality assurance framework, ensuring standards that employers and institutions worldwide recognise.

An Australian degree travels well. That's the point.

*** 02. More choice than almost anywhere
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: Campus variety — modern architecture, labs, outdoor learning
:END:

1,100+ institutions. 20,000+ programs. From 10-week [[/services/education/english-foundation][English language courses]] to doctoral research, [[/services/education/vet-tafe][vocational training]] to executive education — Australia offers a pathway for every ambition, every level, every budget.

*** 03. Work while you study — and after
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: Young professional in office setting, Australian city background
:END:

Australia actively encourages [[/services/migration/student-visas][international students]] to work. After graduation, the [[/services/migration/graduate-work-visas][Post-Study Work Visa (Subclass 485)]] gives you real time to build your career:

| Qualification   | Post-Study Work Visa |
|-----------------+----------------------|
| Bachelor Degree | 2 years              |
| Master Degree   | 3 years              |
| Doctoral Degree | 4 years              |

The Graduate Work Stream provides 18 months for graduates with skills on the relevant occupation lists. Either way, it's a genuine [[/services/career][career launchpad]].

*** 04. A lifestyle that's hard to beat
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: Perth coastline, outdoor lifestyle, café culture
:END:

The 2025 EIU Global Liveability Index placed three Australian cities in the world's top 10. Melbourne fourth. Sydney sixth. Adelaide ninth. The factors assessed: stability, healthcare, culture, education, and infrastructure. Australia performs well across all of them.

*** 05. Affordable compared to the alternatives
:PROPERTIES:
:IMAGE: City comparison graphic or cost-of-living infographic
:END:

Australia offers competitive tuition fees across its university sector — from AUD $20,000 per year for many undergraduate programs. Perth and regional cities offer a lower cost of living than Australia's east coast capitals. The combination makes a meaningful difference over a two to four year degree.

Why Perth.

*** Body

Perth is Western Australia's capital and Blue Education's home base. It's also one of the most underrated study destinations in Australia — four globally ranked universities, a thriving job market across mining, technology, healthcare, and education, and a liveability ranking that consistently places it among the world's top 15 cities.
