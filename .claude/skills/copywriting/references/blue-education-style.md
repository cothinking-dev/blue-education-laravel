# Blue Education — Copywriting Style Guide

## Source of Truth

Always read `@DOCS/docs/` before writing. These are the canonical fact files:

- `company-overview.org` — history, team, credentials, stats
- `brand-messaging.org` — differentiators, tone of voice, audience pain points
- `education-services.org`, `migration-visa-services.org`, etc. — service-specific facts

Never invent stats. Never claim something that isn't in the docs.

---

## Voice & Tone

**Professional but plain.** Write like a trusted advisor who has seen it all and doesn't need to impress anyone.

- Confident, not aggressive
- Empathetic, not sentimental
- Honest, not self-congratulatory
- Specific, not vague

---

## What This Voice Sounds Like

### Approved patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Lead with history/evidence | "Since 1998, we've operated on one principle..." |
| Explain the benefit, not just the feature | "No handoffs means no need to re-explain your situation over and over again." |
| Concrete over abstract | "That means telling you when a visa won't succeed, a course isn't the right fit, or a better path exists." |
| Fragment + confirmation | "Globally recognised universities. Right here in Western Australia." |
| Reader-first framing | "Your degree comes with time to use it." |
| Earned credibility | "That's earned the trust of clients from 40+ countries — for decisions that shaped their lives." |
| Varied sentence length | Mix fragments, medium sentences, and longer complex sentences in the same block |

### Rejected patterns

| Pattern | Why | Example to avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Brand-speak taglines | Sounds cliché | "Boutique by design. Honest by principle." |
| Defensive positioning | Apologises for size | "Blue Education is deliberately small." |
| Clichés | Hollow | "We know your name, your goals..." |
| Parallel if/if structure | Too formulaic | "If a pathway won't work, we say so. If there's a better option, we show you." |
| Salesy fragments | Feels transactional | "Get honest advice. Free." / "No obligations." |
| Self-congratulatory honesty | Performative | "...even when it costs us the business." |
| Inter-state comparisons | Unnecessary | "...without the cost of the east coast." |

## Sentence Structure

Vary structure deliberately within every section. No two consecutive sentences should follow the same pattern.

**Good variety:**
> Since 1998, we've operated on one principle: give honest advice. [long, grounded opener]
>
> Every client is assigned a dedicated advisor who coordinates your education, visa, and career plan — start to finish. [medium, detail]
>
> No handoffs between agencies means no need to re-explain your situation over and over again. [plain English explanation]
>
> That means telling you when a visa application won't succeed, a course isn't the right fit, or a better path exists — before you commit to the wrong one. [concrete + consequence]

**Poor variety (avoid):**
> We help students. We support careers. We handle visas. [all short, parallel]

## Target Audience Framing

Primary readers: mature couples, working adults, and recent graduates (Malaysia, Indonesia, Japan, Taiwan, Singapore) making high-stakes decisions about their future.

Their fears:
- Getting biased advice from commission-driven agents
- Wasting time or money on the wrong pathway
- Being passed around between agencies
- Children's safety and welfare
- Language and cultural barriers

Write to address these fears — not by naming them explicitly, but by demonstrating the opposite through specifics.

---

## Western Australia Focus

Per client direction: frame everything around Western Australia, not generic Australia.

- Reference Perth by name
- Name WA institutions (UWA, Curtin, Murdoch, ECU, TAFE WA)
- Use "golden state of education" framing when appropriate
- Do NOT compare WA negatively to other states

---

## CTAs

Avoid transactional language. Avoid "No obligations", "Free consultation", "Act now".

Prefer: What the reader actually gets, stated plainly.
> "Talk to us. We'll tell you exactly where you stand."

Not:
> "Book a free consultation today. No obligations."
